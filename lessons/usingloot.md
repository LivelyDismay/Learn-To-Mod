# Setting Up LOOT for Magnum Opus / Tempus Maledictum

Hello again. As you may or may not have heard, using Loot on a Wabbajack modlist is generally a bad idea. I won't go into the specifics about why; suffice to say that Loot is a tool like any other, and as such, it requires a lot of manual input in order to be useful with its outputs. Lucky for you, I set up a ton of Loot rules for my modlists, and I include all those rules in your modlist installation folder as well.

But if you've ever tried to launch Loot, it probably didn't work without some tedious nonsense, or you just gave up on it when it wasn't instantly fixed. No matter, I am here to provide instructions on how to set up Loot for my lists. Let's get to it.

---

First, launch your Mod Organizer 2 that came with your modlist. In the executables dropdown, select LOOT and launch it.  
![alt text](https://github.com/LivelyDismay/Learn-To-Mod/blob/main/images/loot01.png?raw=true)

---

Once it opens, select `File > Settings...` from the menu along the top.  
![alt text](https://github.com/LivelyDismay/Learn-To-Mod/blob/main/images/loot02.png?raw=true)

---

You'll be greeted with this window. At the bottom, click the button that says `Add new game...`  
![alt text](https://github.com/LivelyDismay/Learn-To-Mod/blob/main/images/loot03.png?raw=true)

---

You'll get this pop-up.  
![alt text](https://github.com/LivelyDismay/Learn-To-Mod/blob/main/images/loot04.png?raw=true)

---

Name it whatever you want (as long as it's not identical to the name of an existing game).  
Change the Base Game to Fallout 4 / Skyrim Special Edition.  
Leave the checkmark alone, it should be as it is.  
The LOOT folder is the part I can't specifically tell you. This is wherever you installed Opus/Tempus on your own machine. So find your Mod Organizer 2, then navigate to `Tools\Loot\Lively's Stuff` and set that as the LOOT folder path, like you see below.  
![alt text](https://github.com/LivelyDismay/Learn-To-Mod/blob/main/images/loot05.png?raw=true)

---

Once you've got that all filled in, click Save. You'll be sent back to this window.  
![alt text](https://github.com/LivelyDismay/Learn-To-Mod/blob/main/images/loot06.png?raw=true)

---

Edit the `Install Path` on your new entry. This should point at your modlist's Stock Game folder, as seen here.  
![alt text](https://github.com/LivelyDismay/Learn-To-Mod/blob/main/images/loot07.png?raw=true)

---

You can now Save and Exit this menu. And also Loot. Yes, close Loot completely and go back to Mod Organizer 2. Failing to do this will make Loot act stupid, and the new game you just created for it to manage won't work properly.  
Back in Mod Organizer 2, go ahead and relaunch Loot. Click the dropdown at the top and you'll see your new game entry here. Click it!  
![alt text](https://github.com/LivelyDismay/Learn-To-Mod/blob/main/images/loot08.png?raw=true)

---

Now Loot should load all the plugins from your current instance of Opus/Tempus, and you have access to all of my fancy Loot rules and groups. Yay!

If there's an interest in learning more about how to use Loot, feel free to let me know. Who knows, I might write a brief lesson on it. Until then, have a good one, and enjoy your new Loot integration!
